Greetings!

My name is Teghan. I'm a journalist with a passion for narrative storytelling and data-driven reporting.

I work at the Tampa Bay Times on the business and health teams. Using words and code, I cover stories relating to cost of living, poverty, public health and much more.

I have a master's degree from the University of Missouri School of Journalism, where I was a graduate research assistant for IRE and the National Institute for Computer-Assisted Reporting. In this role, I provided data analysis services for newsrooms across the country, which entailed everything from data cleaning to developing story direction with reporters. I also worked for the Columbia Missourian , contributed to the Mizzou-based Ag & Water Desk , and worked as a fellow for the Watchdog Writer's Group, a program that assists journalists with book-length reporting projects. It was a busy two years!

Before grad school, I was a staff reporter for the Pittsburgh Tribune-Review, covering local government, education and of course, the endless facets of the COVID-19 pandemic.

I'm comfortable with R and SQLite, and I am learning Python. I am also a nerd for talking about things like narrative, story structure and the craft of writing.

In my free time, I love to run absurd distances in all kinds of weather (I've done three marathons!), bake and get lost in bookstores.
